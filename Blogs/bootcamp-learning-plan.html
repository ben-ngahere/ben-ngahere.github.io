Foundations: Learning Plan


What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long term goal is get into a remote back-end Web Development position. At the end of this programme, I want to be able to approach a junior web dev position and confidently apply for it (even without the overall experience)

Strengths and Limitations when it comes to learning

I'm a natural problem solver and enjoy challenges. I feel this will me along my learning journey and beyond. I also good with sticking things out and being persistent. These two strengths have help me achieve some good things both personally and in my career before now. One limitation for me is attention span as there's many things at home where I study currently and is easy to start doing other things besides what I'm supposed to be doing (mostly making and eating food). If I find things to be boring, I also tend to get distracted easily

What skills (non-technical/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I'd like to see myself be a bit more open to the course and being online. I've been reasonably reserved, but as we progress through the sprints and beyond, I'm wanting to contribute more to the group. My personal online social presence is very limited as it is, so this will be a big move for me. Even doing these blogs and having them somewhat public is somewhat daunting

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme

I have a set schedule for study and my own time and will stick to this religiously. I'm the kind of person who needs this structure else i'd flounder without it. I've been able to work with many different people over the years, so working together with others is no problem. We're also here to better ourselves so we should encourage each other to do our best and keep getting back up and carrying on when it gets tough, as well as lend a helping hand. When things are getting tough, I need to speak up. I have a bad habit of not asking for help and just tend to dig deep in order to get things done (not always the best thing to do). I'll know ahead of time if i'm unable to achieve a task, so I really need to ask for help then and not at the end of the week or sprint

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

To walk the talk. Help when required, but not give the answer. Be available throughout the study hours and to provide feedback. Keep being awesome humans you all have been so far!

Any scheduling information?

At the moment I only have a few pick-up and drop-offs for my daughter to school and back, but other than that I have no other commitments that will impede my study-time. I do have a casual contract with one of my previous employers, but I have control over how that can look so i'm only committed to that when I say I can be
