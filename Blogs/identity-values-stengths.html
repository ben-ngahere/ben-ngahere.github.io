Identity: Values & Strengths


Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now

In my previous Retail life, I was met a couple times with situations where ethics vs. business policy (in some cases retail law) came about. Most of these times occurred when customers were wanting to return an item. Now bluntly, returning an item due to change of mind generally isn't an accepted reason for return. This was the decision that had to be made almost every time. The one time I didn't go this way and actually gave the return was because of the situation the customer had found themselves in. This has got nothing to do with our policy but I made the decision because it was the right thing to do and Integrity to me is everything. The customer was genuine and the product was un-opened and a popular item that we could have easily resold (which we did later that week). I got a slight reprimand from my boss for not following policy, but he had to say that in order to "tell me off". He too would have done the same thing so I've never felt that this was incorrect, even if my boss didn't agree, I still would stand by what I did

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity

I come from a rather large family (I have 7 siblings) and alot of them live locally. My parental dynamic saw my mum raise us the most whilst working and dad doing long hours with the truck driving - he'd leave very early in the morning and not get in until after we'd be asleep. We spent alot of time at my grandparents (mums parents) house and this would be where alot of old school lessons were taught. Mum had somewhat of a christian upbringing and this has influenced alot of how I am today too. Whilst not religious, I'm grateful for being taught these kinds of lessons. Being Maori and Niuean has also influenced me massively. Biggest lesson I learnt here was to give, even when you can't and not placing too much value on material things

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey

I'm good with puzzles and enjoy the challenge it brings. This is similar for me with coding and problem solving so I think this will help me to stay interested and want to continue to learn. I also get very dedicated once I've found something I want to do. I stay persistent (maybe borderline obsessive?) until I achieve what I've set to do. Having tested these strengths over the years I've spent in Retail will allow me to create a learning plan that I know I'll adhere to

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

Trying to learn too much or going too deep into a topic is where I can fall over. Especially with this "flipped-classroom" model this course has been designed around. I need to sty aware of this so that I don't feel unsatisfied with what I achieve. I also tend to get impatient with learning, more so if i'm not getting it as fast as I think I should be. It's ok that I'm not good at this and will get better with practice

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why

There were 5 of us put into a group to merchandise an area for a sale we were about to have. Lots of product to move and display as well as setup the counter and a way for people to pay. At first we each decided what we wanted to do and away we went. About 30mins into working, 2 of the team members were stuck on how to do something, they sought help and proceeded to carry on. 10mins later they're stuck again and the person who helped them last time wasn't available. The rest of us tried to pitch in and help but none of us were able to really get them to understand, so they floundered for another 10mins before deciding they can't do it and would leave. After trying to help them again, I asked them if they actually wanted to do the task they were set. No was their answer, so team members ended up swapping jobs, funnily they swapped to what they wanted to do and all went well in the end. Instead of letting it be or allowing us to choose the jobs would be something I'd change next time. Utilising strengths and willingness to achieve a goal is what I learnt that day
