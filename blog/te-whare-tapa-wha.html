Te Whare Tapa Wha



Developed by Sir Mason Durie in 1984. This model describes, from a Te Ao Maori perspective, Health and Wellbeing and is represented as a Wharenui/Meeting House with 4 walls

These walls represent:

  • Taha Wairua

    Spiritual Wellbeing


  • Taha Hinengaro

    Mental and Emotional Wellbeing


  • Taha Tinana

    Physical Wellbeing


  • Taha Whanau

    Family and Social Wellbeing


The 4 walls represent 4 dimensions and the Wharenui is the symbol. It also has connections to the Whenua (Land, Sea and Natural World) which forms the foundation for the 4 dimensions


Wellbeing Plan
Walls Actions
Taha Wairua
  • Practice Mindfulness at least 5mins per day
  • Start a Gratitude Journal
  • Write in that Journal once a week
Taha Hinengaro
  • Making sure I take breaks when things get tough
  • Spend more time with my friends
  • Spend time away from the computer
Taha Tinana
  • Spend more time doing walks and runs
  • Make better choices when preparing food
Taha Whanau
  • Have dinner with my parents once a week
  • Catch up with my siblings more often
