Foundations Reflection


What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

  • Perseverance

    • I thought I had this down. There's been a few times in my life where this has had to take the front seat, so am used to it. But during Sprint 4 it was tested. I don't think I've been challenged mentally like that, so my Perseverance got redefined

  • Time Management

    • Now this hasn't been a train-wreck or anything crazy, but I had noticed I started moving away from timers. Not sure if I need to get back on it, but I've started to manage my time based on dividing the total workload over what I think I can do it in? For me it's more intuitive to do it like this. I will be keeping an eye on it as it may not be conducive moving forward

  • Communication

    • This has changed a lot for me. Coming from my immediate background, there'd be multiple colleagues and customers everyday and communication was always on in full (tone, body language etc.) The last 5 weeks makes me feel like I haven't communicated at all, even though I have. Almost feel out of place with it, like I don't know how to anymore. An adjustment is in progress

What are the roles of Values, Empathy, and Self-Awareness in Learning and Programming?

  • Values

    • They act as principles that shape our Motivations, Decisions, and how we Approach tasks. They help to improve our Perseverance and Resilience

  • Empathy

    • An ability to Understand and share the Feelings of others, is surprisingly important to Learning and Programming. It helps to better our Communication, enhancing Collaboration resulting in improved Environments

  • Self-Awareness

    • An ability to Understand our own Emotions, Strengths & Weaknesses and their impact on others is Fundamental. It allows us to manage our Emotions, helping to relieve Stress and be more Adaptable

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

How relevant the skills really are in life in general. I don't think there are many situations, both personal and professional, where these skills wouldn't be of benefit

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

Letting my guard down a little and talking about myself. Definitely not a conversation starter I keep up my sleeve. I've always found that difficult

Why do you think we, a Programming School, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a Web Development BootCamp course?

I think it's because they go hand-in-hand with each other. Coding skills are great and all, but if you can't communicate and work with another person or group, then that's an awkward time for everyone. I believe that these skills were also lacking in the Tech Industry, so they're necessary to make us more employable and be better Developers and Humans in general

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practice programming instead?

No not really. Sure my coding skills definitely need practice, but without the Human Skills, that practice will only get me so far. To me it's like that saying "If you want to go fast, go alone. If you want to go far, go together"
