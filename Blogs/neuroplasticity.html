Neuroplasticity


How understanding the Principles of Neuroplasticity benefits people?

A popular belief is that there are 10 principles of neuroplasticity [Journal of Speech, Language, and Hearing Research, Jeffrey A. Kleim and Theresa A. Jones] Understanding these principles helps benefit people by recognising that there are specific things that can be done to improve intelligence or improved cognitive ability. Once this is recognised, people are able to take control as they no longer feel it's a "natural-only" thing. This leads to believing in ones self and the start of a growth mindset


How would I engage with the Principles?

Of the 10 recognised principles, there are 3 that resonate with me

  • Use it and Improve it

    • Neural paths grow stronger the more they are used. To me and my coding it means getting better at what I already know. This could be practicing the code, reading and talking to others about their code and learning new code to improve existing knowledge

  • Time Matters

    • It takes time dependent on how complex the new behavior/skill is. To me this rings true and is one of the hardest things to want to recognise and be patient with. Whilst wanting to "know it all", learning coding is hard (if it were easy, we'd all be doing it) and recognising that it takes time will help you feel better about the progress that's made, which makes you keep going

  • Repetition Matters

    • Consistency is the key to building new pathways. Practice makes permanent. Like learning anything, practice, practice and practice is required. There's really no two ways about it and for me, recognising this gives me motivation to want to do it more. It doesn't have to be for the longest time, just consistent. Eg. 30mins everyday Vs. 3hrs one day a week - the same amount of total time is taken, but you've engaged 7 times versus the 1 time. 7 small wins versus 1 big win. Consistently achieving multiple small wins often leads to more sustained progress and motivation


What are some ways to increase Neuroplasticity?

Research brings back many ways to do this, but I'm fond of a few of these ways for me to increase my neuroplasticity

  • Play Video Games

    • I do this anyway (as many of us do!) and I'm a big believer that games (not all, but majority) are great for increasing problem-solving, memory, hand-eye and visuospatial co-ordination, reasoning, co-operation and decision making to name a few. There is a difference from using it as a tool to improve cognitive ability versus just playing, so keep that in mind

  • Travel

    • This helps with cognitive flexibility, inspiration and creativity. Experiencing new places and cultures can and has helped me to become a better communicator and broaden my view on the world and life, which has given me different perspectives for either personal growth (goals, relationships etc) and values

  • Learn a new Language

    • Arguably doing this right now. There's research to suggest that by doing this, it increases your brain matter. Increased brain matter helps improve attention, memory and emotions which in turn allows for better problem-solving, increased comprehension and an improved ability to multitask


Growth Mindset


What is it? Why is it Relevant?

A Growth Mindset is believing abilities can be developed through effort. It fosters a love for learning and empowers people to control their development. It's relevant because, as students, it allows us to realise that what we are learning can in fact be learnt and that it's ok if you don't get things right away as this type of mindset will make you persevere at the problem. This is an important state of mind for a coder to have as problem-solving will be a daily occurrence in the workplace. Having this type of mindset is conducive to success personally and professionally
