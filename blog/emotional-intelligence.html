Emotional Intelligence



What is Emotional Intelligence?

A number of sources define EQ as being an ability to understand ones and others emotions as well as manage ones own. People capable of doing this are able to communicate well, build and maintain relationships easily and proceed through challenges. For a web developer, this ability has its merits when working in a team as well as the many challenges we'll come across. It also extends into our personal lives allowing us to become happier about ourselves and be more self-aware of how we are in the world


How is EQ different to IQ?

EQ (Emotional Quotient) is a measure of your ability to understand and manage emotions, empathy towards others and relationship building. IQ (Intelligence Quotient) is a measure of of your ability to problem-solve as well as analytical and logical reasoning skills. Both measure different things, but both are required to be successful in almost any situation. You can liken this to "Book-Smart" Vs. "Street-Smart" and how a combination of both are required


Why is EQ important?

There are quite a number of benefits that can occur both personally and professionally from people who have high EQ or are working towards improving their EQ. Some of these include; - Improved relationships - Increased resilience - Better communication - Better teamwork - Improved conflict resolution There are a few more, but essentially there's next to no downsides to being emotionally intelligent. It benefits yourself as well as others which only equates to success
